Sirr Has Verifiable Security

Sirr uses the OpenPGP encryption standard to provide end-to-end encryption for all user communications. OpenPGP was born in 1991 when governments began mass surveillance of electronic communications. Its founder Zimmermann's goal was: to enable ordinary people to use military-grade encryption to protect communication freedom. Later, the core ideas of PGP were organized into an open standard that belongs to no company or government, maintained by global developers, with the open standard being RFC4880/4880bis. OpenPGP does not rely on central servers, does not need CA, each person owns their own keys and is responsible for them. Encryption happens on the client side, keys are never uploaded to servers, the server is just a "blind courier", even if all servers are compromised, attackers can only see encrypted gibberish. OpenPGP's algorithms are public, protocols are public, and have undergone continuous scrutiny by the global cryptographic community for over 30 years. This is the source of verifiable security. Even if one day Sirr no longer exists, users can still decrypt their historical messages. Therefore, Sirr is a system designed for long-term, serious, and secure communication.
